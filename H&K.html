Hiragana and Katakana

What are Hiragana and Katakana?

Hiragana and Katakana are the two foundational phonetic writing systems (syllabaries) of the Japanese language, together known as kana. Each system has 46 basic characters representing the same set of sounds, but they differ in usage and visual style.

  • Hiragana (e.g., あ, き): Characterized by rounded, cursive strokes. It is used for native Japanese words, grammatical particles (e.g., は, が, を), and word endings (conjugations).
  • Katakana (e.g., ア, キ): Characterized by sharp, straight, and angular strokes. It is used for foreign loanwords (e.g., kōhī for coffee), onomatopoeia, and emphasis.
